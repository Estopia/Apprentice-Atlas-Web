# Editorial governance

Every public item has an author, reviewer, sources, market, audience, social image,
alt text and review date. German and British-English versions are independent
documents connected by `translationGroup`; either can be drafted, published or
withdrawn without forcing the other state.

Publishers verify claims against primary sources, check the country scope, test all
links and confirm photography licence metadata. Editors may draft and translators
may create market versions; only publishers and admins publish or delete. Review
system guides at least every six months and time-sensitive rules within 30 days of
an official change. Corrections are triaged through
`corrections@apprenticeatlas.com` and documented in the relevant version history.

Do not publish invented testimonials, partner logos, user counts, outcome figures,
AI accuracy claims or regional SEO pages without unique reviewed content.

## Minimum editorial depth

A launch-library resource is not publishable merely because it has a title,
summary and generic next steps. Each market version must contain at least five
distinct sections, 240 substantive words, a concrete example or method, an
actionable checklist and at least two relevant HTTPS sources. Product, audience,
trust and company pages require at least five sections and 125 substantive words
per language. Automated content tests enforce these floors; editorial review must
still judge accuracy, usefulness, tone and whether the page says something unique.

Generic claims such as “explore your options”, “AI supports you” or “we care about
privacy” need an operational explanation: what the user does, what the product
does, what it deliberately does not do and how a mistake is corrected. Reading
time is calculated from the rendered bilingual content rather than assigned as a
marketing number.

Pre-launch legal drafts remain `noindex`, stay out of the sitemap and carry a
visible draft notice until qualified review is complete. They do not count as
launch-ready content.
